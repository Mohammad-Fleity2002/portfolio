Artificial Intelligence - Network Intrusion Detection System (AI-based NIDS)

Abstract

Physical awareness emerges as a decisive factor in advancing AI-based Network Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS). While prior studies have largely focused on traffic-level features or algorithmic refinements, the structural properties of IoT networks,their topology,remain underexplored. This thesis provides empirical evidence that incorporating physical awareness through graph-structure information, particularly centrality-based node features, significantly improves the robustness and accuracy of NIDS.

To demonstrate this, we establish a unified benchmarking framework that enables fair and reproducible evaluation across two widely used datasets, CIC_IDS_2017 and CIC_TON_IOT. Within this framework, all major AI model families,machine learning, deep learning, and graph neural networks (GNNs),are systematically implemented, optimized, and compared under consistent conditions. Unlike previous works that assess only one model type, our approach applies graph-structured (physically aware) representations to every model category, revealing their true comparative strengths.

The results show that integrating network topology consistently enhances detection performance across all models. Hybrid architectures such as CNN–LSTM and CNN–GRU achieve state-of-the-art results on CIC_TON_IOT, while MLP and BiLSTM models excel on CIC_IDS_2017. Beyond higher accuracy and F1-scores, physical awareness notably improves minority attack detection and reduces false alarms, key metrics for reliable, real-world deployment

By coupling physical awareness with standardized benchmarking and full-spectrum AI model comparison, this thesis delivers both methodological rigor and practical insight: next-generation NIDS should move beyond traffic-level analysis and embrace the structural reality of the networks they protect.

Research Scope & Acknowledgments

Research Scope

This thesis presents a comprehensive survey that leverages modern standardized datasets, offers balanced coverage of ML, DL, and GNN approaches, and places particular emphasis on topology-aware evaluation. By systematically examining how the integration of network structure,through graph measures and GNNs,impacts detection robustness, generalization, and attack attribution, this work aims to deliver a more holistic and practically relevant assessment of intrusion detection systems in IoT environments.

Acknowledgments

This research was carried out between the Lebanese University – Faculty of Science and the CESI LINEACT Laboratory (France) from May 2025 to October 2025, under the supervision of Dr. Zakariya Ghalmane, Dr. Rami Tawil, and Dr. Mortada Termos. The full thesis report is available for download below.

Key Contributions

  • Topology-Aware Evaluation: Demonstrated that incorporating topology-aware analysis and the physical structure of IoT networks enhances intrusion detection performance.
  • Cross-Model Comparison: Benchmarked models that leverage network topology information against traditional ML, DL, and GNN approaches under unified experimental settings, highlighting the impact of graph-structured features on accuracy and minority class detection.
  • Unified Benchmarking: Presented a fair and consistent evaluation of ML, DL, and GNN models using identical datasets (CIC_IDS_2017 and CIC_TON_IOT), metrics, and experimental procedures.
  • Comprehensive Taxonomy: Proposed a detailed taxonomy of intrusion detection systems categorized across multiple dimensions and analytical perspectives.

Results & Impact

  • Topology-Aware Models Outperform Traditional Methods: Incorporating the physical structure of IoT networks consistently improved detection performance across both CIC_IDS_2017 and CIC_TON_IOT datasets.
  • Enhanced Robustness and Reliability: Graph-aware architectures such as E_GraphSAGE (sum) and E_GAT significantly reduced false positives and false negatives compared to traditional ML models, demonstrating stronger resilience in IoT environments.
  • Superior Deep Learning Performance with Node Features: DL models enriched with node-level features achieved F1 scores above 86% on CIC_TON_IOT and near-perfect accuracy (up to 99.83%) on CIC_IDS_2017, bridging the gap toward GNN-based methods.
  • Unified and Reproducible Benchmarking: Established the first consistent evaluation framework comparing ML, DL, and GNN models using identical datasets, metrics, and procedures, setting a new standard for fair benchmarking in IDS research.
  • Real-World Relevance: Findings emphasize fairness toward minority attack classes and account for operational metrics like false alarms and computational cost, ensuring practical deployability of AI-based NIDS in complex networks.
